The combination of wireless technology and Africa is often considered a natural fit due to historically poor service from monopolistic PTTs, coupled with poverty that favors quick and affordable solutions. Arguments suggesting that Africa requires a different approach due to its uniqueness should be scrutinized. Embracing fiber optics in Africa is advocated to dispel the misconception that Africa is fundamentally different and must embark on a costly journey of self-discovery. Lessons from other parts of the world can be leveraged to enrich Africa's telecommunications landscape without assuming that Africa requires a bespoke set of rules.
